# Salt Editorial — design philosophy for TQ Pool Services

## The movement

**Salt Editorial** is the editorial restraint of a printed magazine crossed with the
quiet pride of a North Queensland tradesperson. It is the photograph of a clean
pool taken at 7am — the light low, the surface still, the materials honest, the
hand of the maker visible everywhere but never announced. Salt Editorial is what
a country newsroom in Townsville would have looked like if the staff had lived
beside the Coral Sea and read Massimo Vignelli on the train. Nothing decorative
exists for decoration's own sake. Every mark is in service of legibility, weight,
and the calm authority of competence.

The philosophy is anti-marketing and anti-templated. Where AI defaults reach for
the gradient, the rounded square, the cheerful emoji, Salt Editorial reaches for
the half-tone, the asymmetric corner, the unhyphenated headline that breaks where
it wants to break. The work must look as if it has been laboured over for hours
by a typographer at the height of their craft, and yet appear effortless — the
restraint of someone who has chosen what to leave out.

## How it manifests

**Form and space.** Generous negative space, an editorial column that does not
fill its container. Sections breathe with vertical rhythm built on a 4-point base,
and shift between full-bleed and contained at deliberate moments. No section is
the same height as its neighbour; rhythm is variable, like water. Asymmetric
corner radii (e.g. `14px 4px 14px 4px`) appear as a recurring micro-signature —
the hand of a tradesperson, not the press of a machine.

**Color and material.** A water-blue spine carries the system; warm sand grounds
it; a single sun-accent appears as the punctuation mark of an emergency stop —
once per screen, never twice. Surfaces are matte, not glossy. The accent is
earned by hierarchy and is never sprayed across multiple elements in the same
viewport. Where AI design defaults to purple gradients, this work defaults to
the colour of clean tile and of paper that has lived near the coast.

**Scale and rhythm.** Display type sets at the largest optical sizes Fraunces
can speak in — confident, slightly condensed, optically tracked. Body type
holds at a single quiet voice in Inter Tight, with the line measure capped at
roughly 60 characters so the eye can rest. Numerals are tabular wherever they
appear in lists, prices, statistics — the ledger of a tradesperson, not the
display of a billboard. Eyebrows are thin, widely-tracked, and short, used as
section markers the way a newspaper uses a department label.

**Composition and balance.** Layouts favour asymmetry over centred safety. A
two-column section is rarely 50/50; it is 1.4 to 1, or 3 to 2, the way a
magazine spread balances image and caption. Pull-quotes and figure captions
are first-class citizens, treated with the same rigour as headlines. White
space at the edges of the canvas is always larger than feels comfortable —
that excess is the proof of confidence.

**Visual hierarchy.** A section can have one headline, one eyebrow, one
illustrative element, and one call to action. Anything more is noise. Where
multiple ideas must coexist, the page sequences them rather than stacks them.
The reader is trusted to scroll. Visual interest comes from the rhythm between
sections, not from cramming each section with detail.

## Craftsmanship

The work must read as if it took countless hours. Every alignment, every
opening of letter-spacing on a display word, every choice of where a headline
breaks, every decision about which corner gets the asymmetric tell — these are
the marks of someone at the absolute top of their field. The reader will not
consciously notice these details; they will simply feel that the site has
been made with care, by hand, for them. Master-level execution does not
announce itself, but it cannot be faked.

Refinement, not addition. When in doubt, remove. When tempted to add a new
flourish, refine an existing one. The discipline of the philosophy is to keep
the composition meticulously crafted and quietly authoritative — the surface
of a clean pool at first light.
